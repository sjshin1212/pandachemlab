Organobismuth Hydride Cleavage (DFT)

Correlating Bi–H cleavage energetics with ligand π-acceptor capacity to guide low-toxicity catalyst design

DFT (M06L/def2-TZVP)
Level of theory
QUAO · CPCM
Analysis & solvation
Ligand set
π-acceptor/σ-donor sweep
Design heuristics
Main-group catalysis

Background

We explore how ligand architecture modulates homolytic Bi–H bond cleavage in molecular bismuth systems, aiming to derive practical rules for greener main-group catalysis.

Methods

  • DFT: M06L/def2-TZVP with CPCM solvation.
  • Orbital analysis: QUAO-based descriptors to probe π-acceptor/σ-donor effects.
  • Sweep: Substituent series spanning –F, –OCH3, –CH3, –NO2…

Results (in progress)

  • Early trend: ligands with stronger π-acceptor character lower the effective Bi–H cleavage barrier.
  • Descriptor set (QUAO) tracks with computed BDEs; candidates surfaced for follow-up refinement.

Impact

The workflow provides quick screening heuristics for low-toxicity catalytic motifs and reduces trial-and-error in main-group catalyst design.
