Computational Investigation of Organobismuth Catalysts: A Green Chemistry Approach

Research Overview. Our research explores organobismuth compounds as sustainable alternatives to toxic and expensive transition-metal catalysts. While transition metals dominate industrial catalysis due to excellent redox cycling, they pose environmental and health concerns. Bismuth offers a compelling solution: affordable, abundant as a byproduct of copper and tin refining, and remarkably safe‚Äîused even in medications like Pepto-Bismol.

The Challenge. Despite bismuth‚Äôs promise in mediating reactions like radical polymerization, hydrogenation, and plastic degradation via hydride attack, organobismuth catalysts remain underexplored. The key difficulty is stabilizing the bismuth‚Äìhydrogen (Bi‚ÄìH) bond, often unstable above 0 ¬∞C. The Bi(III)/Bi(II) redox cycle, involving homolytic Bi‚ÄìH cleavage, is strongly ligand-dependent‚Äîyet the detailed ligand effects remain unclear.

Our Computational Approach

Toolkit

  • Geometry Optimization: M06-L with def2-TZVP in benzene (implicit) to approximate experimental conditions.
  • QUAO Analysis: Quasi-Atomic Orbitals to quantify bond strength, electron distribution, and hybridization.
  • Systematic Screening: Substituents ‚ÄìF, ‚ÄìNH2, ‚ÄìOCH3, ‚ÄìCH3, ‚ÄìSCH3, ‚ÄìNO2 across positions and bridges.

Objectives

Use Hartree‚ÄìFock and DFT to map how ligand architecture governs Bi‚ÄìH stability and catalytic efficiency. We compute homolytic Bi‚ÄìH bond dissociation energies (BDEs) to assess redox cycling capability.

Key Variables Under Investigation

Substituent Types

Electronic donation/withdrawal (EDG vs EWG) and their effects on Bi‚ÄìH stability and reactivity.

Positions

Ortho, meta, para arrangement on the ligand framework to tune sterics and electronics.

Bridging Groups

Linkers such as ‚ÄìCH2, ‚ÄìS, ‚ÄìSO2 modulating rigidity and orbital overlap.

For each configuration, we calculate Bi‚ÄìH BDE to quantify the energy required for homolytic cleavage‚Äîan indicator of efficient redox cycling during catalysis.

Broader Impact

  • Reduce dependence on hazardous transition metals.
  • Lower catalyst costs for industrial processes.
  • Expand the toolkit for green chemistry applications.

By correlating orbital features with bond strengths, we aim to establish predictive models for rapid catalyst discovery. If QUAO profiles reliably predict BDEs, researchers can screen thousands of candidates in silico before synthesizing the most promising systems.

The Vision. Toward greener, safer, and more affordable chemistry‚Äîfrom polymer production to environmental remediation.
