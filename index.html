Background

Hydrogenation of furfural can divert into undesired furan/THF routes via C‚ÄìC scission. We probe atomic-scale mechanisms to explain selectivity differences between Pt(111) and PtSn alloy surfaces.

Methods

  • VASP DFT for initial/final state relaxations.
  • NEB for activation profiles and kinetically feasible routes.
  • Surface models: Pt(111) vs PtSn with distinct H binding characteristics.

Key Results

  • Pt(111): strong H binding ‚Üí C‚ÄìC scission routes accessible ‚Üí byproducts.
  • PtSn: Sn donation weakens local H binding and raises barriers (~2 eV) for unwanted routes, while keeping carbonyl hydrogenation favorable/selective.

Impact

Mechanistic basis for alloy-driven selectivity; informs catalyst design for greener processes.

Background

PLA-PHB blends offer biodegradability with tunable mechanics, but increased crystallinity often trades off with toughness and processing ease.

Approach

  • Literature mining & normalization of reported properties across compositions.
  • Simple mapping of crystallinity vs. tensile metrics with processing notes.
  • Heuristic recommendations for application-oriented targets.

Key Takeaways

  • Moderate PHB fraction improves crystallinity while maintaining workable toughness ranges.
  • Nucleation/annealing protocols shift property windows ‚Üí processing discipline matters.
  • Shortlists of blend windows for packaging vs. durable biodegradable goods.
