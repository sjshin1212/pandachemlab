Computational Analysis of Byproduct Formation: Furfural Hydrogenation

VASP/NEB pathways analysis and selectivity of Pt vs PtSn catalytic surfaces

VASP DFT & NEB
Pt vs PtSn Catalytic Surfaces
Mechanistic Studies
Catalyst Surface Selectivity

Background

Hydrogenation of furfural, a process used to convert biomass into useful chemicals, can sometimes produce unwanted byproducts like furan and THF due to breaking carbon‚Äìcarbon bonds. We use computational simulations such as VASP DFT and NEB to study the reaction at the atomic level and explain why pure platinum (Pt) and platinum‚Äìtin (PtSn) alloy surfaces favor different reaction pathways, helping us understand which surface produces more of the desired product

Methods

  • VASP Density Functional Theory (DFT) for relaxations of initial and final states and thermodynamic analysis
  • Nudged Elastic Band (NEB) for reaction coordinates and kinetically feasible routes
  • Surface Models: Pt(111) vs PtSn with distinct H binding characteristics

Key Results

  • Pt(111): strong H bindings ‚Üí C‚ÄìC scission routes accessible ‚Üí byproducts
  • PtSn: Sn donation weakens local H binding and raises barriers (~2 eV) for unwanted routes, while keeping carbonyl hydrogenation favorable/selective

Impact

Electronic modification by Sn explains the improved selectivity of PtSn catalysts, providing a mechanistic basis for designing alloy surfaces that minimize byproduct formation in biomass upgrading
