Background

Furfuryl alcohol is industrially valuable, but hydrogenation can divert into undesired pathways (furan/THF) via C‚ÄìC scission. We probe atomic-scale mechanisms to explain selectivity differences between Pt(111) and PtSn alloy surfaces.

Methods

  • VASP DFT for initial/final state relaxations (ŒîH selectivity screen).
  • NEB to obtain activation profiles and identify kinetically feasible routes.
  • Surface models: Pt(111) vs PtSn with hydrogen binding characteristics.

Key Results

  • On Pt(111), strong H binding facilitates C‚ÄìC scission routes ‚Üí byproducts form readily.
  • On PtSn, Sn donation weakens local H binding and raises barriers for unwanted routes (~2 eV range), while keeping carbonyl hydrogenation favorable/selective.

Impact

Provides a mechanistic basis for alloy-driven selectivity in biomass-derived molecule upgrading and informs catalyst design for greener processes.
